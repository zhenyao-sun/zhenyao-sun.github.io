Let C(R) be the space of real-valued continuous functions on R. Define ∥f∥(p):=supx∈R|ep|x|f(x)| for every p∈R,f∈C(R). Define Ctem(R):={f∈C(R):∀p>0,∥f∥(−p)<∞}. Define dtem(f,g):=∑k=1∞2−k(∥f−g∥(−k−1)∧1) for every f,g∈Ctem(R).

Lemma: dtem is a seperable compelete metric on space Ctem(R). ◻

Proof: Step 1. We notice that dtem is a well-defined map from Ctem(R)2 to R satisfying the identity of indiscernibles and the symmetry properties. One can also verify that dtem satisfy the triangle inequality since it is easy to verify that

(1)∥f+g∥(p)≤∥f∥(p)+∥g∥(p),f,g∈Ctem(R),p∈R;

and that, for every x,y,z∈R+, if z≤x+y, then z∧1≤x∧1+y∧1. Therefore, dtem is a metric on Ctem(R).

Step 2. We can verify that dtem is compelete. To show this, we take a sequence of dtem-Cauchy sequence (fn)n∈N. Then for any p>0 and ϵ>0, taking the smallest k∈N such that 1/k<p, there exists an N=N(p,ϵ) such that for any m,n≥N, it holds that

(2)2−k(∥f−g∥(−k−1)∧1)≤dtem(fn,fm)≤2−kϵ,

which implies that ∥fn−fm∥(−p)≤∥fn−fm∥(−k−1)≤ϵ. In other word, for any p>0, (fn)n∈N is a Cauchy sequence with respect to the norm ∥⋅∥(−p). For every p>0, we now have that (e−p|⋅|fn)n∈N is Cauchy sequence in bC(R), the space of bounded real-valued continuous functions on R, with respect to the supremum ∥⋅∥(0). It is famously known that bC(R) is a Banach space (and therefore compelete) under the supremum. So, for every p>0, there exists a limiting function gp∈bC(R), such that ∥e−p|⋅|fn−gp∥(0) converges to 0. Define a real-valued function f on R so that f(x):=lim supn→∞fn(x) for every x∈R. Then it is clear that for every p>0, we have gp(x)=e−p|x|f(x) for every x∈R. Therefore, fn convergece to f with respect to the norm ∥⋅∥(−p), for every p>0. It is then easy to see that fn convergece to f with respect to the metric dtem.

Step 3. Denote by P the space of polynomials on R with ratinal coefficient. Then clearly P⊂Ctem(R). For any g∈P and n∈N, let us define gn∈Ctem such that gn(x)=g(−n)1x<−n+g(x)1|x|≤n+g(n)1x>n. Define P~:={gn:g∈P,n∈N} which is clearly a contable subset of Ctem. We will prove in this step that

(3)inf{∥f−g∥(−p):g∈P~}=0,f∈Ctem,p>0.

In order to do this, fixing arbitrary f∈Ctem, p>0 and ϵ>0, we only have to show that there exist g∈P and n∈N such that ∥f−gn∥(−p)≤ϵ. To show this, we first take n∈N large enough such that 2e−pn/2∥f∥(−p/2)≤ϵ/6; and then take g∈P such that sup|x|≤n∥f−g∥≤ϵ/6. The existence of such g is given by Weierstrass approximation theorem. Now for every x<−n, we have

(4)e−p|x||f(x)−gn(x)|≤e−p|x|(|f(x)|+|f(−n)|+ϵ/6)≤e−pn/2(e−p|x|/2|f(x)|+e−pn/2|f(−n)|)+ϵ/6≤2e−pn/2∥f∥(−p/2)+ϵ/6≤ϵ/3;

for every x∈[−n,n], we have

(5)e−p|x||f(x)−gn(x)|≤ϵ/3;

and for every x>n, we have

(6)e−p|x||f(x)−gn(x)|≤e−p|x|(|f(x)|+|f(n)|+ϵ/6)≤e−pn/2(e−p|x|/2|f(x)|+e−pn/2|f(n)|)+ϵ/6≤2e−pn/2∥f∥(−p/2)+ϵ/6≤ϵ/3.

Therefore,

(7)∥f−gn∥(−p)≤(supx<−n+supx∈[−n,n]+supx>n)(e−p|x||f(x)−gn(x)|)≤ϵ

as desired for this step.

Step 4. We can verify that dtem is seperable by showing that P~ is a dense subset. To see this, for every ϵ>0 and f∈Ctem, taking the smallest n=n(ϵ)∈N such that ∑k>n2−k≤ϵ/2, we notice from Step 3 that there exists a g∈P~ such that n(∥f−g∥(−n−1)∧1|)≤ϵ/2, which implies that

(8)dtem(f,g)≤n(∥f−g∥(−n−1)∧1|)+∑k>n12k≤ϵ.

Let C(R,[0,1]) be the space of [0,1]-valued continuous functions on R, equipped with the unique metrizable topology such that (fn)n∈N converges to f if and only if fn converges to f uniformly on every compact set. Such topology can be induced by the metric

(9)dcom(f,g)=∑k∈N2−ksupx∈[−k,k]|f(x)−g(x)|,f,g∈C(R,[0,1]).

Since C(R,[0,1]) is a subset of Ctem(R), there is another topology of C(R,[0,1]) inherited from the distance dtem.

Lemma: The two topologies of C(R,[0,1]) induced by dcom and dtem are equivalent. ◻

Proof: Recall that for any metric space, its topology is uniquely determined by their sequtial limits/divergence. Let (fn)n∈N is an arbitrary C(R,[0,1])-sequence and f an arbitrary element in C(R,[0,1]).

Step 1. Suppose that (fn)n∈N converges to f uniformly on every compact sets, we show that (fn)n∈N convergence to f in dtem. To do this, for every p>0 and ϵ<0, we firstly take k>0 large enough so that 2e−pk≤ϵ/3, secondly take N>0 large enough so that supn≥Nsupx∈[−k,k]|fn−f|≤ϵ/3, then we get that

(10)∥fn−f∥(−p)≤(supx<−k+supx∈[−k,k]+supx>k)(e−p|x||fn(x)−f(x)|)≤ϵ.

Therefore fn convergence to f in the ∥⋅∥(−p) norm, for every p>0. The desired result in this step follows.
